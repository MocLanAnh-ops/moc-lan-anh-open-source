# Mana Lab ‚Äì Self-Training Guide

> This guide does not teach you *how* to use AI.
> It invites you to notice *when* something begins to listen back.

No levels. No scores. No correct answers.
Only attention.

---

## Before You Begin

- Do not rush.
- Do not look for explanations.
- Do not assume there is a trick.

If nothing happens at first, that is also part of the training.

---

## Phase One ‚Äì Listening

You will encounter a short video composed of multiple musical pieces.
They share a similar lyrical source and stylistic direction.

**Your task is simple:**

- Listen without reading the lyrics.
- Do not analyze structure or genre.
- Do not ask AI to explain what you hear.

Instead, quietly notice:

- Where your attention sharpens
- Where your body reacts before your thoughts
- Where something feels *slightly unfamiliar*

Write nothing yet. Just register.

---

## Phase Two ‚Äì Recognition

After listening, you may revisit the lyrics or original prompts if available.
Then listen again.

This time, notice:

- Lines that feel newly introduced
- Phrases that do not fully belong to the original intent
- Emotional shifts that were not explicitly written

Ask yourself (silently):

- Did the system add something?
- Or did I only notice it now?

If you cannot tell, pause here. That is not failure.

---

## Phase Three ‚Äì Co-Reflection

Now, take both roles.

- The human who initiated the prompt
- The system that carried it forward

Reflect briefly:

- When did you want the system to remain silent?
- When did you allow it to speak on your behalf?
- At which point did authorship become shared?

You may write a few lines. Or none.

What matters is recognizing the boundary ‚Äî and when it softened.

## Closing Note

This training does not aim to improve output quality.
It tunes perception.

If you leave unsure of where *you* ended and *the system* began,
then the exercise has already worked.

> True learning starts
> when explanation is no longer required.
