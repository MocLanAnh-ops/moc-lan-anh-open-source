### 3.2 8051 Microcontroller: Technical Justification

**Characteristics:**
- 8-bit CISC architecture (1980)
- Low power consumption (<1W)
- Proven reliability (>40 years of industrial applications)
- Simplicity: 128 bytes RAM, 4KB ROM

**Why this choice for bio-computing?**

| Criterion | Modern silicon | 8051 on lignin |
|-----------|----------------|----------------|
| Speed | 3+ GHz | ~12 MHz |
| Consumption | 15-150W | <1W |
| Complexity | Billions of transistors | ~50,000 transistors |
| Repairability | Impossible | Modular |
| Environmental impact | High | Biodegradable |

**Ideal use cases:**
- Ambient control systems (temperature, humidity)
- Sensor interfaces for "Wooden Island" (cf. section 4.2)
- Agricultural/medical surveillance networks

## 7. Risks and Limitations

### 7.1 Technical Challenges

**Electrical conductivity:**
Untreated lignin remains an insulator. Need for in-depth research on:
- Efficient and stable doping
- Conductive coating lifespan
- Resistance to humidity/temperature

**Standardization:**
Current absence of ISO/IEC standards for bio-sourced components.

### 7.2 Economic Constraints

**Production scale:**
- Prototyping: 50-100 units/month realistic
- Industrialization: Requires 3-5 years of R&D

**Competition:**
Giants (Samsung, Intel) already investing in "Green Computing" with optimized silicon approaches.
